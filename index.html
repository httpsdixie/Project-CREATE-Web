The Program

About Project CREATE

A community-based literacy initiative guided by the Read-Create Approach.

What is Project CREATE?

Project CREATE is a community-based literacy program that promotes reading comprehension, creativity, and reflective learning among children through the Read-Create Approach.

Led by youth volunteers of the VEST Club (Values Education and Spiritual Transformation Club), the program serves children aged 8–13 in communities across Ormoc City — using storytelling, guided reading, and hands-on creative activities to build confidence and a genuine love for learning.

Young volunteers serve as mentors, reading partners, and facilitators, living out the principle: "For the Youth, By the Youth, With the Youth."

"Train up a child in the way he should go." — Proverbs 22:6
"Education is the most powerful weapon which you can use to change the world." — Nelson Mandela

Why Functional Literacy Matters

While the Philippines has a basic literacy rate of about 93%, functional literacy sits lower at around 70–75% based on the 2019 FLEMMS by the PSA. Many can read and write, but still struggle with comprehension, critical thinking, and applying what they read in real life.

That gap is where volunteer action becomes important. Children learn better through interactive and creative methods — storytelling, guided reading, and hands-on activities — which are the core of Project CREATE.
